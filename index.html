Stock Market Game

Manage a simulated portfolio of $10,000 for four weeks and win up to $100!

Registration

  1. Go to Investopedia Stock Simulator, and register for an account.
  2. On the “My Home” page, click on “Add/Edit Games”
  3. Click “Join Game”
  4. Search by “Game Name” and type in “WPI”
  5. Find “WPI IA 2016 Campus Game” and click “Join” on the right side
  6. When prompted for a password, enter “Goatbuck$”
  7. Have fun! The game runs March 21st - April 22nd.

Rules

  • Late entries are allowed – join anytime!
  • Margin trading short selling, and options trading are allowed.
  • Cannot sell securities within 15 minutes of purchase.
  • No single security can make up more than 50% of initial capital.
  • No single options contract can make up more than 10% of initial capital
  • Complete rules can be found here.
  • Individual portfolios, trades, and rank are visible to all players.

Prizes

(In Amazon Gift Cards)1: $100, 2: $75, 3-5: $50, 6-10: $25, 11-20: $10.

Automated Trading Competition

Use an online Python platform to build, backtest, and trade using algorithms.

Submission

  1. Go to Quantopian, and click "Start Coding" to jump right into your first algorithm!
  2. Use the Quantopian Lecture Series or our other resources for reference.
  3. Submissions are due at 11:59pm on Sunday, March 27.
  4. Each WPI student is allowed up to 3 submissions (different algorithms); keep in mind that only the best-performing submission for each student will be counted.
  5. To submit, email iaexec@wpi.edu with your algorithm files in the form [username]_[submission number].py (for example, jsmith@wpi.edu would submit files named jsmith_1.py, jsmith_2.py, and jsmith_3.py).

Rules

  • Submitted algorithms must compile error-free on Minute backtesting mode.
  • Algorithms will run using Quantopian Paper Trading (simulation based on live data) for 4 weeks, 3/28-4/22, and will be judged on final portfolio value.r
  • The use of leverege is prohibited, except in cases of short selling. An easy way to ensure this is to use the order_target_percent() API method.
  • Submissions will remain confidential.

Prizes
